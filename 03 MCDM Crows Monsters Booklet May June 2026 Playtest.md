# MCDM Crows Monsters Booklet — May/June 2026 Playtest

This book contains creature stats meant for the ref to use, though players may access the animal stats for use with spells like animal form.

> **Playtest Note:** The current list of monsters includes just what is needed for the playtest: some animals, some blood creatures, and one unique creature. More creatures of all types will be in future tests.

## Monsters

The term "monster" is used in this game to refer to creatures who are not humans or animals. There are six monster types in the core game:

- **Angels:** Though called angels for their otherworldly home and beauty, these creatures are far from heavenly. They radiate magic that charms and warps the minds of their prey.
- **Blood Creatures:** Amalgamations of flesh and fluid of humans and animals, blood creatures are held together by the same magic that makes them ravenous for the flesh of other creatures. Most are animalistic in nature.
- **Demons:** Beasts summoned from the pits of Hell, demons slaughter other creatures for fun. They're fast, strong, and tenacious.
- **Plants:** Animated by necromancy, these plants use viney tendrils and roots to move, stab, and choke prey to quench a literal bloodthirst.
- **Undead:** The reanimated remains of humans, twisted into killing machines that abhor anything alive.
- **Uniques:** Some monsters in this book are unique experiments of the necromancers. Most have a level of sapience the other monsters lack and there exists only one of each such creature in Cornath.

## Monster Names

Except for uniques, each monster doesn't have a specific name. Instead, a monster is given a letter after it's type for referencing in the rules, like "blood creature A" or "undead C." The arrival of monsters threw the world into chaos, so there is no universal nomenclature for them.

Each monster has art for you to show the players, so they can come up with names for them. There's a list of colloquial names that NPCs might use to refer to a monster so the Ref doesn't have to come up with them on the fly (but they still can).

## Creature Stats

Most creatures have the same stats as PCs with the following exceptions.

### Slots

Monsters don't have slots and die when they are reduced to 0 Stamina. Humans and pet animals have the potential to be allies, so they do have slots, which counts as backpack slots for them.

If a PC or other creature with backpack slots gains the stats of a different creature, they keep their original number of backpack slots.

### Power

A creature's power signifies their general toughness and threat to other creatures. In this booklet, a creature's power is scaled from 0 to 50, though future products could go even higher! The greater the power, the more deadly head-to-head combat is with the creature. The more experienced the PCs are, the more likely they are to survive an encounter with a creature of high power. Starting PCs stand no chance in a head-to-head battle against creatures of power 11 or greater (and even creatures of power 1 or more in groups can really mess them up).

### Darkness No Penalty

Monsters have special senses that mean darkness and dim light impose no penalty on them. If a human or an animal has a similar trait, it is called out in their stat block.

### X/Rest

So creature's have a feature that has "X/Rest" written next to it. The creature can only use this feature X number of times between rests.

# Blood Creatures

Craelin created blood creatures—amalgamations of fluid and flesh of several creatures brought together in the form of an entirely new predator. Blood creatures have exposed musculature and red features. They know on some instinctual level that their existence is unnatural, and this knowledge drives them to feast on the flesh of humans and animals as a way to prove they are superior and belong in the world.

## Blood Creature A

**Common names:** creep, red drool, spindleclaw

These blood creatures hunt in packs, dropping from above onto prey. They drool bright red saliva when food is near.

# Ring Collector

The Ring Collector is a creature gifted with supernatural strength and speed who is obsessed with finding and collecting magic rings. They invaded and wiped out several villages during the Necromancer War in pursuit of rings.

The Ring Collector is Namlin, a farmer who lost his family to a fire during the war. He knew of a legendary ring that could bring people back from the dead and went about finding as many magic rings as he could. Each warped him. One even erased his memory, but not his obsession to find magic rings, and so now he has no purpose beyond the hunt.
