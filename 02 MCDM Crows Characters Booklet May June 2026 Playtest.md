# MCDM Crows Characters Booklet — May/June 2026 Playtest

## It's a Living

It also might be a dying, but you knew the risks when you became a crow. This book contains the rules for players to create and advance their crows, their equipment, and their village. Options here refer to rules in The Rules booklet.

## Crow Creation

To create a crow, follow these steps.

### 1. Roll For Background

You weren't always a crow. Your background informs the statistics and equipment your bring with you when you first enter the profession. To get a background, roll 2d6 on the Backgrounds table. The first die result is the left d6 column of the table and the second is the right d6 column.

### 2. Record Statistics

Record your background's statistics on a piece of paper with a pencil. Each background gives you a +1 bonus in some skills, a Stamina score, and a starting trait from a trait tree (a full description of each trait is found in Traits). All PCs have a starting speed on 5.

#### Characteristic Adjustments

Your characteristics start at 0. Your background raises one to +1. Sometimes it gives you a choice of which characteristic to raise. You can raise an additional characteristic to +1, but if you do, you must lower the characteristic left at 0 to -1.

#### Name and Feature

Give your PC a name and a distinguishing feature. This could be a tattoo, distinct body odor, unique voice, strong personality trait, or the like. Just give them one thing stand out.

### 3. Equipment Cards

Your background also gives you a set of starting equipment. Every PC has a bedroll, an empty coin purse, a knife, a rope, and six rations.

### 4. Make a Village

If this is your group's first time making PCs for a campaign, work with other players to make a village and name it (see The Village).

## Advancement

Whenever you return to your village (see Village) and finish a rest after looting a dungeon, you gain experience points (XP) equal to the combined value of all the treasure your group brought back in gold coins divided by the number of players (not characters) in the party. Unique items don't have a cost. Their XP value is 10,000, or 500 if it can become useless after use.

You can spend XP on new traits (see Traits). You should also keep track of the total XP your PC gains over the course of their adventuring career (called total XP or TXP).

The Skill and Stamina Advancement table shows the TXP required for your PC to increase their skill bonuses or Stamina. When you get a bonus, choose one of the following options:

- Increase two skill's bonuses (including skills in which you don't currently have a bonus) by 1 (to a maximum of +2).
- Increase your Stamina maximum by 4.
- Increase one skill's bonus (including skills in which you don't currently have a bonus) by 1 (to a maximum of +2), and increase your Stamina maximum by 2.

### Skill and Stamina Advancement

| TXP | Bonus |
|---|---|
| 100 | 1st |
| 500 | 2nd |
| 1,250 | 3rd |
| 2,250 | 4th |
| 3,500 | 5th |
| 5,000 | 6th |
| Every 5,000 after | 7th and up |

The Characteristics Advancement table shows the TXP required for your PC to increase one of their characteristics by 1, to a maximum of 3. If all of your characteristics are 3 when you get this bonus, you instead gain 4 Stamina.

### Characteristics Advancement

| TXP | Bonus |
|---|---|
| 5,000 | 1st |
| 15,000 | 2nd |
| Every 15,000 after | 3rd and up |

### New PC After Death

If your PC dies and you make a new PC to take their place in the same campaign, you can roll on the Backgrounds table an additional number of times equal to the dead PC's number of bonuses on the Skill and Stamina Advancement table and choose any background for the new PC from the results you get.

### Starting With More

If all of the other crows in the party have 5,000 or more TXP when you create a new PC, your character lags significantly behind the others in terms of power and survivability. This imbalance is a fun challenge for some players to offset with caution, equipment, and clever tactics. The disparity doesn't last long. The new PC gains XP fast with experienced crows. For other groups, the new PC is a burden that leaves some folks feeling annoyed or ignored. To offset this unevenness, the Ref can decide that a new PC starts with XP equal to the lowest TXP of a crow already in the party.

It's unlikely that a new crow will need better equipment than what they start with, since their dead counterpart likely left gear behind that they'll get, but the Ref can give the PC gold pieces equal to half their starting XP to be used to buy better equipment. This money must be spent on equipment and not upgrading village institutions.

## Traits

Traits are character options that enhance your PC's ability to create and use equipment. Your background grants you a trait. Traits are themed and grouped in trees.

### Buying Traits

Whenever you finish a rest, you can spend XP to buy one or more traits. You can only purchase a starting trait on a trait tree or a trait connected by a line to another trait you already have on the same tree. Starting traits appear at the top of trait trees and cost 500 XP each. You can only purchase a trait once.

### Minimum Modifier

Whenever a trait increases or decreases a number equal to one of your characteristics, the minimum number of that increase or decrease is 1, even if your characteristic is lower.

## Alchemy

*For creating and using acid, poisons, potions, and bombs*

### Midnight Oil

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- When you work on crafting an alchemy item as part of the Craft Equipment rest activity, you can make two crafting rolls for the item or make a second crafting roll for a different alchemy item.

### It Burns

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- Acids and poisons you craft deal additional damage equal to twice your Mind. Bombs you craft deal additional damage equal to your Mind.

### Potent Potion

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- Healing potions you craft restore additional Stamina equal to your Mind or remove 1 additional wound if the drinker is already at full Stamina.

### Alchemy Epiphany

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- When you make a crafting roll for an alchemy item, you get a crit when the dice without modifiers equal 18, 19, or 20.

### Big Boom

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- Bombs you craft have the size of the cube they effect increased by 1.

### Lasting Brew

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- Potions and poisons you craft that have a duration listed in UD gain 1 additional UD.

### Eschew Ingredients

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- You can ignore the material requirements for crafting an alchemy item if the item's crafting goal is 100 or lower.

### You In Particular

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- When you use a bomb, choose one creature who must make the RR against its effects. That creature takes -2 penalty to the RR against the bomb.

### Potion to Gas

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- As an action, you can turn a potion you carry into a gas. The potion is immediately consumed and two creatures of your choice within 1 square of you, including you, are affected as if they drank the potion.

### Two for One

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- When you finish crafting an alchemy item, you craft two of the item instead of one.

### Don't Waste a Drop

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- You can use an acid or a poison twice before it is consumed.

### Alchemy Belt

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- You gain a third belt slot that can only be used to hold alchemy items.

## Alteration

*For maximal use of alteration spells*

### Lasting Alteration

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- When you cast an alteration spell with a duration listed in UD, add an additional 1 UD to the duration.

### Grow Scales

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- When you cast an alteration spell, you can harness the magic to grow temporary scales on your body and gain AD equal to your Mind. The AD granted by this trait disappears at the end of a rest.

### Alteration Stone

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- As a rest activity, you can create an alteration stone (which has an equipment card). This stone disappears if you create a new one.

### Alteration Mastery

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- Non-doom tier 1 results of rank 0 and 1 alteration spells you cast don't add to the chaos count. Your alteration spells of rank 2 and higher are treated as 2 ranks lower when they trigger a backlash.

### Hooves

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- When you cast an alteration spell, you can harness the magic to change your feet into powerful hooves until the end of the DT. While you have these hooves, your speed increases by your Mind.

### Morphing Stone

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- A creature who holds the stone can use a maneuver to change it into the shape of a mace, maul, flail, or back to its original form. The stone gains the stats of its weapon form while retaining its own stats.

### Improved Animal

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- When you cast an alteration spell that allows you to turn into an animal, you can turn into an animal with power equal to what the spell says plus your Mind.

### Horns

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- When you cast an alteration spell, you can harness the magic to grow horns from your head that last until the end of the DT. You can make an unarmed attack with the horns as a maneuver.

### Movement Stone

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- Add the following benefits to those your stone can confer:
  - You can breathe underwater and gain a swimming speed equal to your speed.
  - You gain a climbing speed equal to your speed.

### Split Alteration

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- When you cast an alteration spell that has a target of 1 creature, object, ally, enemy, or target, you can target one additional qualifying target.

### Wings

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- When you cast an alteration spell, you can harness the magic to grow wings that last until the end of the DT. While you have these wings, you gain a flying speed equal to your speed.

### Spell Stone

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- Choose up to three alteration spellbooks in your possession. The stone absorbs and destroys those books. A creature wielding the stone has those spellbooks equipped. The stone can only hold three spells.

## Armor

*For getting the most out of protective wear*

### Interposing Arm

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- When you wield a shield with at least 1 AD and a creature makes a weapon attack against a creature adjacent to you, you can use your reaction to cause the damage to apply your shield before the target.

### Stalwart

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- While wearing armor, the distance you can be force moved is reduced by 1, and you can't be knocked prone by tier 1 and 2 attack results or by tier 2 and 3 RR results.

### Stand Strong

- **XP Cost:** 500 (Starting)
- While you wear armor, you gain a +1 bonus on Strength RRs.

### Hurl Shield

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- You can make a ranged weapon attack with your shield. This attack is a Strength test that is ranged 5 and deals 3 + S damage on a tier 2 result and 6 + S damage on a tier 3 result.

### Sacrifice Armor

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- When an attack reduces armor you wear or a shield you wield to 0 AD and there is still damage left, you can reduce the remaining damage to 0. If you do, the armor reduced to 0 AD is destroyed.

### Headgear

- **XP Cost:** 1,000
- While you wear armor or a magic item in your head slot, you gain a +1 bonus on Mind RRs.

### Wall of Protection

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- While you have a shield equipped, you gain a +1 bonus on Agility RRs. When you make an Agility RR while wielding a shield, you can use a reaction to grant a +1 bonus to adjacent allies on the same RR.

### Immovable

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- Creatures of your size can't force move you or knock you prone with mundane attacks or the Knockback maneuver while you wear heavy armor.

### Jury-Rig Repairs

- **XP Cost:** 1,500
- You can quickly repair a piece of armor while you are not in combat, restoring 1d6 AD to it. You can't use this trait again until you finish a rest. This trait stacks with Emergency Repairs from the Blacksmithing tree.

### Shield Bash

- **XP Cost:** 2,000
- When get a tier 3 result on the Knockback maneuver and force move the target while you have a shield equipped, the target is also knocked prone.

### Second Skin

- **XP Cost:** 2,000

### Armor Enchantments

Armor enchantments can be added to suits of armor or shields. The Armor Enchantments table shows the Enchanting skill bonus, materials, and crafting goal required to enchant the armor and if the enchantment can be placed on a shield, suit of armor, or both. It also shows the price for buying the enchantment. If you buy armor with an enchantment on it, add this price to the armor's base price.

If a suit or armor or a shield has a single enchantment that requires no more than a +1 Enchanting bonus to craft, it can receive a second and different enchantment that requires no more than +1 Enchanting bonus to craft. Otherwise only mundane armor can get an enchantment.

Some armor has unique enchantments that aren't listed here. The information for crafting that armor is listed on the item's card.

The following enchantments require a +1 Enchanting bonus to craft:

- **Demon's Head:** A demon's head is emblazoned on both sides of this shield. While you wield it, you deal additional damage with a counter equal to your Agility or Strength (your choice) as the outer face bites your target. When you take damage from a counter, you take an additional 1 damage as the inner face bites you.

- **Feather:** While you wear this armor, whenever you fall, the damage is reduced by 3d6. When you are force moved, you can be moved 1 additional square.

- **Glow:** While you wear or wield this armor, you can use a maneuver to make it glow, giving off light 5/5. This effect has 2 UD and the UD recharge when you finish a rest. Once you turn this effect on, it can't be turned off until the UD expire. If you roll a doom while wearing, wielding, or carrying the armor and the light effect is available but hasn't been turned off, the effect activates.

- **Heavy:** While you wear this armor and are force moved, you reduce the move by 1. If you are knocked prone, you must use your action to stand up instead of a maneuver.

- **Luring:** When another creature hits you with a melee attack while you wear or wield this armor, you can use a reaction to slide them 1 square. If a creature gets a crit against you with an attack, you can't use this enchantment's reaction until the end of the DT and the creature can slide you 1 square.

- **Silent:** While wearing this armor, you gain a +1 bonus to tests made to hide and sneak. If you get a tier 1 result on a test made to hide or sneak, you are boned.

- **Slick:** This armor secretes a non-flammable oil. While wearing it, you can move 1 additional square when you use the Shift maneuver and you gain a +1 bonus to the Escape Grab maneuver's test. When you roll a doom on an Agility or Strength test, you fall prone.

- **Spell-Storing:** As a rest activity or as part of the Repair Armor rest activity while repairing this armor, you can touch a spellbook to it. While wear or wield the armor, you can cast the spell as if you were holding the spellbook. If you roll a doom on any test while wearing, wielding, or carrying the armor, the spell is immediately cast, and the Ref picks the targets. If there are no viable targets, add 1d6 to the chaos count and the spell is expended from the armor. Once you cast the spell, it is gone from the armor. The armor can only store one spell at a time. When a spell is placed in it, and old spells are gone from the armor.

- **Telepathic Node:** As a rest activity while wearing this armor, you can choose up to five other willing humans who rest with you to become connected to the armor until you use this rest activity again or die. While you wear this armor, any of the creatures connected to the armor can communicate telepathically with each other and you as long as they have line of effect to you and you can communicate telepathically with them regardless of distance or obstacles. If a creature connected to the armor dies, you and the other creatures connected take 1d10 P damage. If you die, connected creatures take 2d10 P damage.

- **Waterwalking:** While wearing this armor, you can walk on the surface of any liquid as if it were solid ground. If you fall prone while standing on liquid, this armor's enchantment ceases to function until the end of the DT and you take a -2 penalty on a tests made to swim.

The following enchantments require a +2 Enchanting bonus to craft:

- **Banishing:** When a creature hits you with a melee attack while you wear or wield this armor, you can use a reaction to push them 1d6 squares. If a creature gets a crit on a melee attack against you while you wear or wield this armor, you fly vertically up 1d6 x 10 feet and then fall and can't use your reaction to activate this armor in response to the attack.

- **Climbing:** While you wear this armor, you gain a climbing speed equal to your walking speed. If you are knocked prone while climbing, you immediately fall and this armor's enchantment ceases to function until the end of the DT.

- **Dancing:** As a maneuver while wielding this shield, you command it to float in the air and follow and protect you, freeing it from your hand slot though you are still considered wielding it, until the end of the DT. A creature who scores a crit against you while the shield is floating can grab it and wield it (no action required), causing the shield to stop floating. The shield can't be made to float again until you finish a rest. You can only have one dancing shield active at a time.

- **Deep:** While wearing this armor, you gain a swimming speed equal to your walking speed and can't suffocate. When you fall while wearing this armor, the fall damage increases by 1d6.

- **Flying:** As a maneuver while wielding this shield, you can place it on the ground and make it hover. While hovering, a Medium or smaller creature can ride on the shield and gains a flying speed equal to their walking speed and counts as wielding the shield. The shield can fly until the end of the DT or until it loses all of its AD, at which point it and any creature using it to fly falls. Any attacks or damaging effects made against the creature flying that originate from below first damage the shield before any other armor the creature wears. The shield can't be made to fly again until you finish a rest.

- **Passthrough:** While you wear this armor, you use a maneuver to move up to your speed. While you move, mundane walls and other obstructions can't hamper your movement. If you end this move inside of a solid object, you are shunted back to the space where you entered it and take 1d6 P damage for each space you are moved through on your way back. This maneuver has a 1 UD that must be rolled after activating the effect. The UD recharge when you finish a rest.

## Weapons

A weapon's equipment card shows how much damage it deals on a tier 2 or 3 result with an attack. It doesn't show the tier 1 result, which is always a miss.

### Weapon Types

A weapon's type determines the skill that can be applied to attacks made with it. A weapon can be a bashing, bow, chopping, slashing, stabbing, or unarmed weapon.

### Weapon Qualities

A weapon can have any of the following qualities:

- **Brutal:** When you get a crit on an attack with this weapon, you deal twice as much damage with the attack as your normally would.

- **Cumbersome:** The weapon only occupies 1 slot on your backpack or belt but requires 2 hand slots to wield.

- **Disengage:** When you take the Shift maneuver while wielding this weapon, you can move 1 additional square as part of the maneuver. If you wield two weapons with this quality, the benefits stack.

- **Dismember:** When you score a crit on an attack made with this weapon, it removes one of the target's limbs. Roll on the Dismember table to determine the limb removed.

- **Light:** When you hit with a melee attack while wielding two light weapons, you deal additional damage equal to the unused weapon's tier 2 result (without the inclusion of your Agility or Strength as part of the damage). While a hand slot is empty, it counts as a light weapon that can be used to make unarmed strikes.

- **Parry X:** While you wield this weapon, you can use it to absorb damage as if it were a shield. The weapon has AD equivalent to X. When the weapon's AD is 0, it takes a -1 damage penalty. You can repair the weapon as if it were a shield using the Repair Armor rest activity.

- **Pummeling:** When you get a tier 3 result with an attack made with this weapon against a creature who is your size or smaller, you can push them 1 square. When you get a crit on an attack with this weapon, you can knock a target of your size or smaller prone.

- **Reload:** You must use a maneuver to load 1 piece of ammunition into this weapon before it can be used to attack. After being used to attack, it must be loaded again before it can be used to make another attack.

### Weapon Enchantments

Weapon enchantments can be added to existing weapons. The Weapon Enchantments table shows the Enchanting skill bonus, materials, and crafting goal required to enchant a weapon. It also shows the price for adding an enchantment to a weapon. If you buy a weapon with an enchantment, add its price to the weapon's price.

If a weapon has a single enchantment that requires no more than a +1 Enchanting bonus to craft, it can receive a second and different enchantment that requires no more than +1 Enchanting bonus to craft. Otherwise only a mundane weapon can get an enchantment.

Some weapons have unique enchantments that aren't listed here. The information for crafting those weapons are listed on the items' cards.

Unarmed weapons can't be enchanted.

The following enchantments require a +1 Enchanting bonus to craft:

- **Absorbing:** When you get a crit on an attack with this weapon against a creature, you can choose one attack from the creature's stat block and make that attack using the creature's stats this turn. When you get a doom on an attack made with this weapon, you can't make attacks with it until the end of your next combat turn.

- **Defending:** The weapon gains the Parry 5 quality or if it already has it, it increases by 5. When the weapon's AD is 0, it takes a -2 damage penalty.

- **Exploding:** When you kill a creature with this weapon, they explode. Each creature within 1 square of them takes 1d6 damage. If the creature already explodes on death, any damage they deal is increased by 1d6. When you get a doom on an attack with this weapon, a mundane item you carry (chosen by the Ref) is destroyed.

- **Impact:** When you get a tier 3 result on an attack with this weapon and the target isn't force moved, you can push the target 1 square. If the target is already force moved by the attack, you can force move them 1 additional square. If you get a doom on an attack with this weapon, the Ref slides you 1d10 squares.

- **Lightning:** When you hit a target with an attack with this weapon, you can choose a second target within 3 squares of the first. That creature also takes the attack's damage. This effect has 1 UD rolled on use and recharges on rest. When the UD are 0, you are boned.

- **Poisoning:** As a maneuver, you can command this weapon to drip poison, an effect that has 1 UD to be rolled on use and which recharges on rest. If the next attack you make with your weapon is a hit, the target is boned. If you miss with your next attack, you are boned.

- **Raging:** When you kill another creature with this weapon, you can use a reaction to enter a rage that lasts until the end of the DT. While in the rage, you gain a +2 bonus to damage on attacks with this weapon and a +1 bonus to speed while you wield this weapon. When the rage ends, you are boned.

- **Slaying:** You choose a specific creature type when you place this enchantment on a weapon. The weapon gains a +1 bonus on attacks against creatures of that type and a -1 penalty on attacks against all other creatures. You can only place one slaying enchantment on a weapon.

- **Sworn Foe:** As a maneuver while wielding this weapon, you can choose a creature within 10 squares of you. You deal an additional 2 damage on attacks with this weapon against that creature. If that creature counters you, you take 2 additional damage. If you choose a new creature, the effects of this weapon no longer apply to the old creature.

- **Vicious:** When you get a crit on an attack with this weapon, you deal an additional 1d10 damage. When you get a doom on an attack with this weapon, you take 1d10 P damage.

The following enchantments require a +2 Enchanting bonus to craft:

- **Dancing:** As a maneuver while wielding this weapon, you command it to float in the air and follow and fight for you, freeing it from your hand slot(s) though you are still considered wielding it, until the end of the DT. A creature who scores a crit against you while the weapon is floating can grab it and wield it (no action required), causing the weapon to stop floating. If the weapon is ever more than 1 square from you, it stops floating. The weapon can't be made to float again until you finish a rest. You can only have one dancing weapon active at a time.

- **Flaming:** As a maneuver while wielding this weapon, you can command it to ignite in a magical fire that gives the weapon a +2 damage bonus and causes it to shed light 5/5. You can use another maneuver to extinguish the flames. When you get a doom on an attack with this weapon, you take 1d10 damage as the weapon suddenly bursts with flame and then its fire goes out and you can't ignite it again until the end of the DT.

- **Frosty:** When you get a tier 3 result on an attack with this weapon against a creature, the creature's speed is reduced by 1. This effect can't reduce a creature's speed below 2. When you get a doom result on an attack with this weapon, your speed is reduced to 2 (if it isn't already lower) until the end of the DT.

- **Gashing:** When you get a tier 3 result on an attack with this weapon against a creature, the creature gains a bleeding gash. The gash deals 1d6 P damage to a creature at the start of their combat turns. If target receives magic healing or the gash's damage result is 6, the gash stops dealing damage to the target. When you get a doom on an attack with the weapon, you get a gash.

- **Hewing:** This enchantment can only be added to non-bow weapons. As an action, you can use this weapon to make a hole in a wall, floor, or ceiling that has a circumference of 1d10 feet and a depth of 1d10 feet. This effect has 1 UD rolled on use and recharges on rest. When the UD are 0, you are boned.

- **Hungry:** When you kill a creature with this weapon, you gain 3 AD. AD gained this way disappears at the end of the DT. If you wield the weapon in a combat encounter and the encounter ends without you having killed anything with it, you take 1d6 P damage.

- **Infinity:** This enchantment can only be added to bow weapons. If you don't load ammunition into this weapon, it generates its own phantom ammunition that can be used to make attacks. The ammunition has a -1 damage penalty.

- **Returning:** This enchantment can only be added to non-bow weapons. - **Weakening:** When you hit a creature with a tier 3 attack made with this weapon and do so again before the end of your next combat turn, the creature is boned. When you get a doom on an attack with this weapon, you are boned.

## The Village

Crows die and retire, but their village remains. With Cornath recovering from near destruction, your village is one of the only places left for humans to survive. Invest in your village, and your community will thrive and better prepare you for delving into ruins. If you ignore your village, it will decay, leaving you without a place to buy equipment, sell treasure, or safely survive your time outside of dungeons.

In Cornath every village is contained within a ruin. These structures are enclosed, protecting the people within from the Miasma and the monsters wandering the wilds.

### Other Villages

As you travel from one dungeon to another, you might stop in a village other than your own to rest or trade. These villages function the same way as your own, except you can't invest in them and the Ref doesn't have to worry about tracking their prosperity or events. The Ref determines the level of each institution in another village.

If you want to invest in more than one village, ask the Ref. You'll need to start tracking Prosperity, events, and all the other stats for that village yourself. It's a challenge to keep up with more than one village. Don't attempt something like this until you have the hang of it with one.

### Starting Village

At the start of a campaign, your village institutions include a blacksmith, crypt, general store, inn, and temple. As a group, choose one other institution already established in your village. All these institutions are 1st level. Then come up with a name for your village as a group.

### Village Cycle

Village cycles track when events occur, when new institutions get established and upgraded, and when merchants restock. A village cycle is 10 days long.

### Prosperity

Your village's Prosperity is a numerical representation of how happy and economically well off the people of your village are. As institutions improve, so do opportunities for the villagers. The higher the Prosperity, the more villagers care about each other, and seek to protect and help their neighbors. If Prosperity falls, the village's infrastructure begins to degrade. With less money coming into the town, there are fewer opportunities for villagers, and many become destitute or turn to crime to live.

At the start of a campaign, your village's Prosperity is 0, which is average. It's not doing amazing, but it's not doing terrible. This number is important in determining item availability (see Trade) and the type of events your village experiences (see Village Events).

#### Raising Prosperity

When you invest the money to found or upgrade an institution, you increase your village's Prosperity by 1. The highest Prosperity a village can have is 10.

#### Lowering Prosperity

When a village cycle ends without any new institutions being founded or upgraded in your village, its Prosperity decreases by 1. The lowest Prosperity a village can have is -10.

### Village Events

At the end of each village cycle, the Ref rolls for an event that occurs during the next village cycle (which happens whether or not the PCs are in the village). This event impacts the village or the PCs in some way. The higher the village's Prosperity, the more likely the event is to be a boon to the PCs. The lower the Prosperity, the more likely the event is to have a negative impact.

When a village event occurs, roll on the Village Event table to determine what happens. Unless otherwise specified, when an institution, item, or PC is referenced by the table, that institution, item, or PC is randomly chosen by the Ref.

> **Playtest Note:** The Village Event table will have more events, thus decreasing the odds for any one event.

### Trade

You can buy or trade goods in your village, provided you have merchants who can supply them. A merchant's description in Institutions lists what the buy and sell.

#### Buying and Availability

When you want to purchase an item from a merchant, you must check to see if the item is available for sale. Find the merchant you wish to purchase the item from and check its availability, which is a percentage number found on the merchant's table in Institutions. Roll a d100, and if the result is equal to or lower than the item's availability on the table plus the village's Prosperity, then it is available. If the item isn't available, you can't attempt to purchase it again from that merchant until a new village cycle. If the item is available and you choose not to purchase it, it is available for purchase for the rest of the current village cycle, and when a new cycle starts, you must roll to see if it is available if you wish to purchase it then. If more than one merchant could sell the item, you can check for its availability at each new merchant.

Unless otherwise noted, items are purchased for the price listed in the rules, either in a booklet or on their cards.

#### Selling

Items can be sold in the village to an appropriate merchant. The money gained from the sale is a percentage of the item's base cost based on the village's Prosperity, as shown on the Sale Percentages table.

| -1 to 1 | 50% |

### Village Crafting

Some institutions, called artisans, provide crafting services for PCs. If the PC can't find a craftable item they want and wish to craft it themselves, they can pay the institution with a high enough crafting bonus to do it.

- To begin the project, the institution must be given the crafting materials for the item and payment, which is the item's full price.
- The institution makes one crafting roll per day toward crafting the item (because they have more than just your business on their plate). The institution's stats include the bonus you should add to this crafting roll. You should keep track of the crafting points.
- If you pay twice as much, the institution makes it a rush job and makes two crafting rolls a day for the item.
- When the item is complete, you can visit the institution and pick it up.

## Institutions

Institutions are the merchants, temples, and other sites, services, and organizations that you can interact with while you are in the town. You should write down and keep track of each institution's level and its steward, which is the person who owns or is otherwise in charge of it. Stewards are NPCs played by the Ref. When you found an institution in your town, the Ref can provide the name and personality of the steward, or they can ask the player whose character invests in the institution to create the steward.

Each institution has the following details:

- **Founding Price:** The price to establish the institution in your village. When you pay to establish an institution, it doesn't start operating until the start of the next village cycle.
- **Services:** The services provided by the institution are detailed here. Some services are only available when an institution achieves a specific level.
- **Level:** Each institution has a level that increases when you pay to advance it. An institution's starting level is 1. Its maximum level is detailed on its advancement table.
- **Advancement Table:** An institution's advancement table shows the price to increase an institution's level, and the benefits that come from advancing the institution. When you pay to upgrade an institution, it doesn't start operating at its new level until the start of the next village cycle. Merchant item availability is shown on this table. When rolling for an item's availability, remember to add your village's Prosperity to the availability before rolling.

### Auction House

**Founding Price:** 2,000 gc

**Merchant**
The auction house has the following services:

- **Buy Any Valued Item:** They sell any item that has a price, including gems and art objects. To figure out the cost of an available item, roll any die. If the result is even, the item is discounted by 1d6 × 10%. If the result is odd, the item costs 1d6 × 10% more.

- **Buy Unique Items:** On rare occasion the auction house sells a unique item, such as a magic horn of which only one exists in the world. ### Crypt

**Founding Price:** 2,000 gc

**Honor the Fallen**
When a crow dies during an adventure, their remains (whatever that may be), can be brought to the crypt and interred there. When the crow is interred, their player picks a boon from the list below. Once per cycle, the dead crow can instill upon another crow who prays at their grave gains the boon. A crow can only benefit from one boon at a time, and only one crow can have that dead crow's boon at a time. If you already have a boon and pray for another, the new one replaces the old one.

Expending a boon requires no action. After you expend the boon, you no longer have it.

- **Boon of Cooperation:** When you make an assist, you can expend this boon to grant an additional bonus to the test you aid equal to twice the crypt's level.
- **Boon of Disappearance:** You can use this boon to become invisible for a number of combat rounds equal to the crypt's level.
- **Boon of Escape:** You can expend this boon to teleport a number of squares equal to three times the crypt's level.
- **Boon of Flight:** You can use this boon to gain a flying speed equal to your speed for a number of combat rounds equal to the crypt's level.
- **Boon of Fury:** When you deal damage with an attack, you can expend this boon to deal additional damage to the target. You deal an additional d6 damage to the target for each level the crypt has.
- **Boon of Greed:** You can expend this boon when you are in a dungeon to learn the direction of the most valuable treasures on the same level as the dungeon as you and how many chambers, including hallways, you must pass through to reach them. The number of treasures you learn about is equal to the crypt's level, in order from most valuable to least.
- **Boon of Knowledge:** When you expend this boon, choose a specific creature, place, event, or organization with a proper name that you know. You can ask the Ref up to a number of questions equal to the crypt's level about the subject, which they must answer honestly.
- **Boon of Rescue:** After you roll a RR, you can use this boon to improve the result by 1 tier. You can use this boon a number of times equal to the crypt's level before it is expended.
- **Boon of Swiftness:** When you expend this boon, your speed increases by a number equal to the crypt's level until the end of the DT.
- **Boon of Vitality:** When you regain Stamina, you can expend this boon to regain additional Stamina equal to twice the crypt's level.

When the crypt is 5th level and the village's Prosperity is 10, it is considered 6th level for boon effects.

### Inn

**Founding Price:** 1,000 gc

**Merchant**
You have a home in your village, so you don't need to stay the night at the inn. However, if you're in another town (or want to stay at the inn in your village for some reason), the cost of a night at the inn, which includes a day's worth of food, is 5 gc.

**Gambling**
As a rest activity, you can gamble at the common room of the inn. The maximum bet you can place is based on the inn's level. Minimum bet is 1 gc. You can play the following games at the inn.

- **Over/Under 7:** State whether you think the result of a 2d6 roll will be over, under, or exactly 7 and make a bet. Then roll the dice. If you were wrong, you lose your bet. If you were right and guessed over or under 7, your money is doubled. If you guessed a result of exactly 7 right, your money is quadrupled.
- **Guess the Number:** You must guess a number from 1 to 6. Then the Ref rolls a d6. If you guessed the result incorrectly, you lose your bet. If you guessed correctly, your winnings are five times your bet.

**Find It Here**
When the inn is 5th level and the village's Prosperity is 10, you can find a special traveling merchant at the inn. Once per cycle, you can purchase any item that has a price from this merchant, but the cost of the item is increased by 25%.

### Your Home

Every player character has a small living space they call home within their village. This house is a secure place to keep equipment and treasure that you keep stocked with food. While you're in your village, you don't need to worry about tracking money to spend on food and shelter.

## Retirement

When your PC has 50,000 or more TXP, you can retire them to the village. If you do, your PC becomes an NPC and no longer delves into dungeons. You can give your equipment to a worthy successor, perhaps a new PC you create or one of the other crows your retired adventurer delved alongside. A retired PC gives up the dangerous life of dungeon delving.

When you retire to the town, choose one of the following benefits that the town gains. If you have 100,000 or more TXP, you can choose two benefits:

> **Playtest Note:** More retirement benefits will be in a future version of the test.

- **Best Steward Ever:** Your retired PC becomes the steward of an institution in the village (taking over from the previous steward). This institution always operates as if the village's Prosperity is 10, even if it is lower.
- **Crow Daddy:** Your retired PC is sure to give helpful tips to new crows. Choose one starting trait your retired PC knows. When you or another player creates a new PC from the village, that PC can start with that trait in addition to any other traits they start with. A new PC with 0 TXP can start with a maximum of three traits.
- **Generous Benefactor:** Your retired NPC still loves to hear stories about the crow life and hangs out at the inn common room or a tavern listening to all the latest adventures. Once per cycle, the PCs can visit your retired PC who gives them a gift rolled on the [Playtest Note: insert table name here ... coming soon].
- **Master Mentor:** Choose up to three traits your retired PC knows. Other PCs can learn these traits from your retired PC, making them cost half as much XP as they normally do.
- **Work With My Hands:** Your retired PC goes to work at an artisan institution in the village. This institution gains an additional +5 bonus to crafting rolls.
